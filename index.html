Martin Luther king jr

Biography

Martin Luther King was a preacher and civil rights activist. He helped lead the Civil Rights Movement in the United States from the late 1950s until his death in 1968. His main goal was to end racial segregation and discrimination in America. King's job involved preaching, speaking out against racism, and organizing protests123. He was the head of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference, which promoted nonviolent tactics to achieve civil rights4. King was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 19644.
